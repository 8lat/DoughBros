Dough Bros | Cinnamon Bliss


Indulge in the warm cinnamon flavor of this sourdough bread, perfect for any meal. Its versatility allows for a variety of toppings and spreads, and can be enjoyed toasted with butter or used as a base for your favorite desserts.



  • Preparation time


    • Total: Approximately 4.2 days
    • Preparation: 4 Days
    • Cooking: 2 hours


  • Ingredients


    • 400 Grams of Whole Grain Flour
    • 100 Grams of White Whole Wheat Flour
    • 365 Grams of Warm Water
    • 9 Grams of Fine Seasalt
    • 6 Grams of Cinnamon
    • 50 Grams of Sugar


  • Instructions


    1. Create the Starter: Firstly, go ahead and mix 25 grams of Whole Grain Flower with 25 grams of Warm Water. Once combined, the starter will begin to ferment, and cause the bread to rise.
    2. Maintaining the Starter: To maintain your starter, keep it at room temperature and feed it daily, to feed it, you must half your starter, Split your starter in half, and either throw it out or make another dough with it. Next, you add the same amount of water and flour, as there are grams in your newly halved starter. Continue this process for 6 days.
    3. Creating the Dough: Whisk the starter and all of your water together in a large bowl, as you mix, begin to add your flour and your salt. Let rest for 30 minutes, and work the dough into a fairly smooth ball.
    4. Bulk Rise: Put the dough into a bowl and cover it with a damp towel. Let the bowl sit overnight at room temperature. (8-10 hours) You will know the dough is ready, when it has doubled in size, you do not need to be precise about it, you should be able to tell.
    5. Shape & Assemble: Remove the dough onto a lightly flowered surface. Let it rest for 10 to 15 minutes. Longer rested this stage will relax it, though, making it easier to stretch into a rectangle. Line a 10 inch oval proofing a proofing basket with a towel and dust with flower combine the sugar and cinnamon into a small bowl. Set side.
      With flowered hand gently stretch the dough into a long rectangle about 16 X8 inches. Lightly brush the surface of the dough with water then evenly sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture over the top, leaving 2 inch porter at the top and bottom edge and a 1 inch border around the side. With the short and facing you roll the dough to a log pinching the end to seal. Place it in the basket.
    6. Rise your Dough: Put your dough in a covered bowl, and let rest for 30 minutes to 1 hour. The dough is ready when it is risen slightly, if your bread hasn't risen after an hour, we recommend you recheck the steps.
    7. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 450Â°F. Before putting the dough in the oven, you must score/put a pattern on the sourdough to ensure it is cooked all the way through.
    8. Bake your Dough: Bake the dough in a Dutch oven, on the center rack of your oven for 20 minutes. (Cover the pot) After 20 minutes is up, remove the cover, and continue to bake for 30 minutes. Then, carefully remove the loaf from the pot.
    9. Finish Cooling: When done, take the loaf out of the oven, and let cool for 1 hour.
    10. Enjoy your Loaf: Plate the loaf, and cut into desired sizes. We recommend serving with Nutella, Bananas, or something pleasant of your choosing.
